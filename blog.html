As a State, we must rediscover one of the basic concepts that have served us so well for so long: a free society with free markets, which has historically produced more possibilities for achievement and more roads out of poverty than any other system in our history.

EDUCATION

Emma feels that education SHOULD be the Civil Rights fight of the 21st century. He believes that access to a better education should not be confined to those with financial means, and he is a strong advocate of school choice. Any and all options must stay available in order for everyone to have a chance at success.

Emma also feels that the more localized education is, the more efficient it is. The Federal Government's role in education should be minimal at best, and parents and local communities should be best equipped to cope with their children's educational challenges.

ECONOMY

Emma is a firm believer in the free market, believing that the government that rules the least governs the best. To create opportunities for ALL Abians, deregulation, tax simplification, and pro-growth policies must be implemented. He also thinks the state's debt should be lowered, and he supports a balanced budget amendment to prevent out-of-control government spending from bankrupting future generations.

Neighbourhood safety and First Responders.

Following an alarming increase in local crime, our feeling of safety in our neighbourhood has shifted radically. I will support and finance our first responders, including police officers, firemen, and ocean safety officials so that they can perform their duties and keep us all safe.

INFRASTRUCTURE AND TRANSPORTATION

Emma feels that the Federal Rail System is underused in our region. He is a big proponent of building a rail system that connects Abia state to other regions of the east, as well as other Nigerian states. This would provide locals with a great alternative to driving and would also help to minimize traffic congestion. He also feels that the installation of truck stops, notably in Umuahia, should be a component of any federal infrastructure improvement plan. These stops would greatly improve the safety of our roads and greatly reduce the need for our truckers to use local streets to find a place to take their Federally mandated breaks.
