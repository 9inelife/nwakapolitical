An Ordinary Family

His parents, Late Mr. and Mrs. Godwill Ezurike Nwaka resided in Amokwe-Amiyi, Oguduasa in Isuikwuato Local Government Area of Abia State. They relocated to their village at the start of the Nigerian civil war.

1964

Emmanuel Chiedoziem Nwaka lost a couple of years of schooling during the Biafran enclave's civil hostilities.

He completed his primary education at Amokwe-Amiyi and completed the same at Methodist Primary School, Emene in Enugu State in 1972. In 1973, he gained admission at St. Theresa's Secondary School, Nsukka but could not stay at the boarding house because of financial constraints.

Emma Nwaka concluded his legal studies in 1982 and was called to the Nigerian Bar in 1983 as a Solicitor and Advocate of the Supreme Court of Nigeria. After his national youth service at the Ministry of Justice, Kano, Emma Nwaka joined the Law Firm of the Late Chief James Uba in Aba in 1984.

Higher Education
Emma was among the first set that sat for JAMB exams in 1978. He did well in the exams and was offered admission to read Law at the prestigious University of Nigeria, Nsukka. Again, the money problem reared its ugly head

What did Emma Nwaka do; he went to the office of the Registrar of NIJ to inform him of his predicament. He pleaded for the partial refund of the fees he paid to enable him to proceed to the university. The Registrar told him point blank that fees are not refundable. Young Emma Nwaka broke down in tears and Mr. Ajayi, the Registrar, had pity on him and gave him some money. It was about 30 years after that Emma found out that it was his personal money that Mr. Ajayi gave to him.

Emma Nwaka concluded his legal studies in 1982 and was called to the Nigerian Bar in 1983 as a Solicitor and Advocate of the Supreme Court of Nigeria. After his national youth service at the Ministry of Justice, Kano, Emma Nwaka joined the Law Firm of the Late Chief James Uba in Aba in 1984.
